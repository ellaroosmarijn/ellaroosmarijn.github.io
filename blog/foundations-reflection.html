Sprint Five

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I have learned that I have a mix of a growth and fixed mindset and that it is something I really want to keep improving), that I really will keep persevering when things are hard, and that through the five rules of neuroplasticity I can improve my memory and learning abilities.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

The role of values in learning and programming is that they can guide how one acts in different scenarios. For instance, if a student or programmer is very stuck on a task they would give up if they didn’t value challenges and perseverance.

Empathy has a strong role in learning and programming, because it dictates how aware you are of other people and team environments. People with more empathy, or a higher EQ, can really improve team dynamics.

I think that self-awareness ties into both values and empathy. It is linked to values because if one is self-aware of their values and how they respond in difficult scenarios they can more easily navigate through them. Moreover, if one is self-aware they may be more inclined to notice when empathy is important or positively impactful in a given scenario.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I have been somewhat surprised by just how intertwined the core learning is with the tech learning. I knew that EQ was important and linked in, but I didn’t realise how impactful a growth mindset and neuroplasticity was on learning in general.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

For me, the most challenging aspects of the core learning was writing down and sharing in a publicly accessible place my values and examples of them in play. I don’t mind discussing these things with someone, but writing them down feels very permanent and I feel as though I have little control over who I share it with, and thus who gets to know something about me. I’m a reasonably private person, so sharing these things was a challenge.
