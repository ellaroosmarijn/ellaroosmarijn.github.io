Identity, Values and Strengths

Sprint Two

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

We are faced with ethical decisions regularly throughout our lives. Sometimes we make the right decision, and sometimes we don’t. Whenever I come across an ethical decision I try to look inwards and reflect back on my personal values.

An example of a recent ethical decision I was faced with was a decision I made with my partner. My partner had a beautiful working-line black German Shepherd called Juno, who we both loved very much. She was roughly a year old when we decided that we wanted to move overseas (to Holland) in a couple of years and travel around the world. We realised that this would be very difficult with a working-line German Shepherd, especially if we lived in an apartment in the central city. We thought that taking her with us would be extremely unfair, particularly when we move into an apartment with no outdoor space. We tried to weigh up our options to try to make it work, for instance we considered the possibility that we could both be able to take her out for a couple of hours a day, but quickly realised that with both of us working and studying full time it would be too much and wouldn't be fair on her. Upon realising that she wouldn’t fit into our future lives very easily we made the decision to pass her onto a family that had children to entertain her and an outdoor space to let her run around in. Making this decision was heart-breaking, particularly for my partner, but we both believe that it was the right thing to do for her.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I believe that the people around you influence who you are and what you value. Over the past few years I have been working on surrounding myself with people who are positive, honest, trustworthy, and hard-working. Since making this change I found that these values became even more important to me, and something that I will always firmly stand by. Moreover, surrounding myself with people encouraged me to act out those values and work harder than ever, which definitely showed in my study and day-to-day life.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

Determined and driven. Once I set my sights on something I will work relentlessly to get there, although I am still very flexible if things change. An example of this would be when I skipped my last year of highschool to attend the University of Auckland and graduate by 19.

In Big 5 tests I tend to score quite high in conscientiousness and open mindedness. I view this as a strength, because it means that I am very open to new ideas and learning new things. Attending Dev Academy is an example of this. Towards the end of my degree I was preparing to do a research masters in political conflict resolution and governance. As part of my preparation I wanted to upskill and learn how to handle data effectively, so I taught myself PostgreSQL - I find learning new things is very exciting. To say that I really enjoyed it would be an understatement, and it is what led me to decide to pursue a career in tech. This change in career path and openness to learning something new is an example of my open mindedness.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

As I mentioned above, I am a very driven and determined person. This comes with many benefits but also has a couple downsides. The main downside for me used to be that I would move very quickly. Whilst I may have gotten a lot done in the day, it did mean that sometimes I would miss a small detail and have to take a little extra time to go back and fix it or re-read the instructions. I’ve found that this hasn’t impacted my learning journey too much, although it has meant that I’ve had to learn to slow down and take my time.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

While I was studying at the University of Auckland I worked part time in sales at a high end retail store. There were many jobs to be done throughout the day, which I really enjoyed because I like to stay busy. Unfortunately my manager would take advantage of that and push most of the jobs onto me, even if it was a job for two people. I approached my manager on the subject, but found that he only pulled his weight for a short period before reverting back to giving me most of the work. When it was clear that approaching him didn’t work I began writing a job list for myself every morning, so it was harder for him to give me more work. If he tried to add more to my list, I would either accept the jobs or tell him that I didn’t have time. I found that this was an effective method because he could more clearly see how much I was taking on and he couldn’t justify giving me more. Because of this I had more control over my day and my workload.
