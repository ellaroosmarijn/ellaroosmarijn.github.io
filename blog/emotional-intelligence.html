What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional intelligence (EQ) is the ability to pay attention to the present moment by identifying and regulating emotions, understanding the emotions of others, and expressing curiosity and a willingness to learn. In short, EQ is the ability to perceive, use, understand, manage and handle emotions in the present moment. There are many important benefits to strengthening your EQ, including help building relationships, reducing stress, defusing conflict, and improving job satisfaction. EQ is not something that people are born with, it is a characteristic that we learn and strengthen. This can be done through mindfulness, self-awareness, sensitivity to others, and conscious listening.

What is the Difference Between Intelligence Quotient and Emotional Intelligence?

Intelligence Quotient (IQ) assesses an individual's human intelligence by measuring their ability to solve problems, use logic, and understand complex ideas. It is helpful for academic work, but does not improve emotional regulation or building relationships. On the other hand, EQ assesses an individual's ability to regulate and understand emotions, and use that knowledge to inform decisions. IQ assesses intellect, while EQ assesses emotional intellect.

Why is Emotional Intelligence Important?

As stated earlier, EQ has many benefits. Let's go through a few of them.

Reduces Stress.

EQ can help with reducing stress. Individuals that can recognise and manage their reactions to other peoples emotions and their own feelings can respond to them positively by staying calm and taking a more objective view of the situation. In a work environment this is a particularly important benefit of EQ, because it helps teams work together under stress.

Improves Reaction to Receiving Constructive Criticism.

Being aware of your emotions and controlling them enables individuals to react positively to constructive criticism. Constructive criticism can be difficult to hear, and may make people defensive because they are responding emotionally. It can create a hostile atmosphere. Through emotional awareness a positive reaction and atmosphere can be maintained.

Improves Communication and Defuses Conflict.

Sometimes tension begins to build either at home, in the workplace, or at school and individuals can struggle to manage their emotions. This may result in an emotionally driven response, which can have a negative impact on the situation at hand. However, with higher EQ the individual would have recognised how they were feeling, took responsibility for it, and calmly communicated what they needed to. This can be the first step to resolving conflict.

Fosters Better Leadership.

Using their EQ, leaders can understand their team's emotions and responses more efficiently. This may mean identifying positive and negative motivators for each team member, and for the team as a whole. This would positively impact the team's engagement and productivity.
